# Case Study Guidelines — Opeyemi Portfolio Redesign

*Synthesized from: NNGroup survey of 204 UX hiring professionals, IxDF case study research, Case Study Club, portfolio analysis (Brittany Chiang, Cuberto, ToyFight, Lynn Fisher, Bruno Simon, HoverStat), and Opeyemi's existing case study content.*

---

## 1. The 5-Second Test

Every case study page must pass: **"What did Opeyemi do, and why should I care?"** answered in 5 seconds.

### Required above-the-fold elements:
| Element | Example | Why |
|---------|---------|-----|
| **Project name** | "Poseidon Design System" | Instant context |
| **The one-line hook** | "A unified design system that ended fragmented UX across 5 fintech products" | Specificity > generality |
| **Role + context** | "Senior Product Designer · HydrogenPay · 2024" | Credibility signal |
| **Outcome preview** | "−40% design-to-ship time · WCAG AA across 180+ components" | Proof before scroll |

### Anti-patterns to avoid:
- Vague taglines: "A design project for a fintech company" → fail
- No outcome in first fold: forcing managers to scroll for impact → fail
- Jargon without context: "Atomic design methodology" without saying what it achieved → fail

## 2. Recommended Case Study Structure

Use **Freytag's Pyramid adapted for UX** (from IxDF):

### Exposition (Hook + Context)
- Project background — one paragraph
- The inciting incident: *why* this project mattered
- Your role and team (specific: "led a team of 3 designers")
- Measurable goal: "Increase conversion by 1.5% and monthly revenue by 3%"

### Rising Action (Research → Design)
- Research methods and key insights (NOT just tool screenshots)
- How insights translated to design decisions
- Show the messy process: sketches, whiteboards, discarded ideas
- **Critical:** "Show what you didn't pursue and why" — NNGroup hiring managers on what they look for

### Climax (The Breakthrough)
- The pivotal moment: usability test insight, stakeholder alignment, technical breakthrough
- The key design decision that unlocked everything
- Before/after contrast — visual or conceptual

### Falling Action (Iteration → Ship)
- What changed after testing
- Implementation challenges and how they were resolved
- Collaboration with developers/stakeholders

### Denouement (Outcomes + Reflection)
- **Quantifiable results** (metrics, KPIs, before/after data)
- **Qualitative impact** (testimonials, user feedback)
- **What you learned** — hiring managers trust you more when you admit mistakes
- **What you'd do differently** — shows growth mindset

## 3. Content-to-Visual Ratio

**Target: 60% visuals / 40% text** (IxDF recommendation)

| Section | Visuals | Purpose |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Hero | 1 hero image or video | Set tone, show product |
| Research | Photos of whiteboards, research artifacts, persona cards | Prove process |
| Design | Wireframes → mockups → final, side-by-side comparisons | Show evolution |
| Metrics | Data visualization (simple: big numbers + labels) | Immediate impact |
| Reflection | None needed | Let text land |

**Avoid:** Wall-to-wall text blocks. Break with images, pull quotes, metrics callouts, and process artifacts.

## 4. Project Title & Hook Formulas

### The Cuberto Formula (Best for client/agency work):
```
Project Name – Value Proposition
"Poseidon – A unified design system for 5 fintech products"
"PhoneCash – Making cashless payments accessible across Nigeria"
```

### The Chiang Formula (Best for personal projects):
```
Action Verb + What + Context
"Building a design system that unified 5 products"
"Designing a mobile wallet for Nigeria's unbanked users"
```

### Hook length: 5–12 words
Longer than 12 words loses scanning efficiency. Shorter than 5 lacks specificity.

## 5. Metrics That Matter

### Hierarchy of impact (strongest first):
1. **Revenue/profit impact:** "Contributed to $80M+ in investor funding secured"
2. **Efficiency/time savings:** "−40% design-to-ship time"
3. **User behavior change:** "+50% user satisfaction, −40% bounce rate"
4. **Scale/reach:** "180+ components serving 5 products"
5. **Quality/compliance:** "WCAG AA across all components"

### Presenting metrics:
- Put the biggest numbers in the hero section
- Use simple callout format: big number + short label
- Include context: "25% uplift in satisfaction" → from what baseline? With how many users?
- Even "failures" have metrics: "We learned X, which informed Y"

## 7. Process Artifacts Checklist

For each case study, include at least 3 of:
- [ ] Research photos/screenshots (interviews, surveys, competitive analysis)
- [ ] Whiteboard sketches / early wireframes
- [ ] Mid-fidelity mockups with annotations
- [ ] Before/after comparison of key screens
- [ ] Usability test results / iteration log
- [ ] Design system artifact (token table, component library screenshot)
- [ ] Collaboration artifacts (Slack threads, Figma comments, developer handoffs)

## 8. Voice & Tone

### What works (from your existing UX Buddy case study):
- Personal, conversational: "Every designer knows that sinking feeling..."
- Honest about struggle: "At one point, UX Buddy kept changing its mind"
- Specific about craft: "I split the plugin into two parts: one that handled the data and another that handled the interface"
- Emotionally resonant: "It felt like having a calm, honest mentor by your side"

### What to keep:
- That conversational voice — it's your differentiator
- Specific technical detail — shows you build, not just design
- Honesty about challenges — hiring managers cite this as critical

### What to tighten:
- Some passages run long without a visual break
- Metrics occasionally buried mid-paragraph — pull them out
- Persona backstories can be 2 sentences instead of 2 paragraphs

---

## 9. Scannability Rules

Hiring managers spend 1-2 minutes on first scan (IxDF). Design for scanning:

- **Bold your key insights** — not entire paragraphs
- **Pull metrics into callout blocks** — never bury them in body text
- **Use section headers that tell the story:** "The Problem" → "What We Discovered" → "The Breakthrough" → "What Shipped" → "What I Learned"
- **One idea per paragraph** — short paragraphs (2-4 sentences)
- **Image captions should be substantive:** "Final dashboard design with real-time analytics" not "Dashboard screenshot"
